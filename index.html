Will the undercut work?

What's actually going on here?

In F1, tyres wear out over a race. As the rubber degrades, the car gets a little slower every lap, the same way old running shoes lose their bounce. At some point every car has to pit: drive into the garage, swap the worn tyres for a fresh set, and rejoin the race. That stop costs time, usually somewhere between 18 and 27 seconds depending on the track, since the car is crawling through the pit lane at a strict speed limit while a crew changes four wheels.

Here's the interesting part. Once a car has fresh tyres, it's faster than a car still running on old, worn ones, often by well over a second a lap. So if you pit a few laps before your rival, you spend those laps lapping much faster than them while they're stuck on ageing rubber. Sometimes you gain back more time than the pit stop itself cost you, and when your rival finally pits too, you come out ahead of them even though they never lost the lead on track. That move is called the undercut.

It's a gamble though. Pit too early and your fresh tyres start wearing out before your rival even stops, so you lose your advantage just as it matters. Pit at the wrong moment and you might come out of the pits stuck right behind a slower car with nowhere to overtake, which erases the whole gain instantly. That's the puzzle this tool lets you play with.
